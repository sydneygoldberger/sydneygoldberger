Mind the Gap: A Visual Archive of the Gender Pay Gap
Parsons School of Design - Fall 2020
Adobe Photoshop, InDesign


Mind the Gap archives the average annual gender pay gap of all women in the U.S. over the last seven decades. On average, in 2019, women in the U.S. were paid 82 cents for every dollar paid to men. This archive does not focus on the 82 cents on the dollar, but instead focuses on the 18 cents left that women have to earn in order to receive equality. My overall intention by creating this archive is that it can help inspire a complete closure of the gap and also serve as a record for society’s progress to a more equal future. Mind the Gap highlights the number of openings that women have already closed as well as the openings that they have yet to close.

Process: I calculated the difference between the pay gap percentage and 100% from 1950 until present day. For example, in 1977, the overall gender pay gap in the US was 58.9%. I therefore represented 1977 with 41.1 blue squares.

Design: The archive is designed as a flip book displaying the movement of the pay gap through the movement of the number of blue squares on the page. The squares are inspired by a calendar: a direct representation of time. The color blue is inspired by risographs - a timeless process that that is not tied to any specific year and consequently spans generations, just like the gap itself.

Captions: I asked 100 women to answer one question: In one word, describe a way to close the gender pay gap in the United States. I used the answers that I received as captions to each entry.
