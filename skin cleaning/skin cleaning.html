skin cleaning
Parsons School of Design - Fall 2020
Figma


According to the Harvard Business Review, "65% of consumers say they want to buy eco-conscious and clean brands, yet only about 26% actually do so." After user research and serveral competitive analyses, I designed skin cleaning, a digital platform to counteract this discrepency in consumer behavior. skin cleaning is a digital platform that makes it easier to buy clean skincare brands, while allowing users to donate back to the earth with their purchases.

Process: After user research and a full competitive audit, I defined the scope of my platform. I took advantage of user testing to help me architect the app's information. I created personas and user flows that helped me form wireframes to define the platform's structure. The last part of my process was to establish the brand's aesthetic. Mood boards, typetests, and gestural designs allowed me to shape skin cleaning's brand identity.

Features: skin cleaning takes advantage of the fact that when it comes to skincare, users are extremely brand loyal. Brand categories and filter tags create an effortles and painless search process for users to find what they need. Each brand will have a detail page that navigates users to the brand website for purchasing. If users purchase products through the app, skin cleaning will track their eco-points, and if they accrue enough eco-points, users can donate their points to one of the listed environmently-focused non-profit.
